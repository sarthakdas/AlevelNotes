Computing Related Legislation

These are laws that can protect personal privacy, interest of national security, crime detection and counter-terrorism.

The Data Protection Act 1998

Anyone who stores personal details must keep them secure. They must put in place security mechanisms.

  • Data must be processed fairly and lawfully
  • Data must be adequate, relevant and not excessive
  • Data must be accurate
  • Data should be retained only till necessary
  • Data must be secure
  • Data should not be transferred

The Computer Misuse Act

There are three main principles, primarily designed to prevent unauthorised access or 'hacking' of programs or data.

These are the following offences:

  • Unauthorised access to computer material
  • Authorised access with intent to commit crime
  • Unauthorised modification of computer material
  • Unauthorised modification of computer material

The Copyright Design and Patents Act 1988

Copyright laws gives the creators of artistic and creative works the right ti chose how their material is used.

  • Anything you create is automatically copyrighted
  • It lasts for a set period of time
  • Does not apply when used for educational purpose
  • Does not apply in order to criticise and report on them

The Regulatory of Investigatory Powers Act 2000

This allows public bodies to carry out surveillance and investigation, and covers the interception of communications. It was introduced to take into account the growing use of internet communication.

  • Public Bodies can demand ISP providers to access to customers communications in secret
  • Enables mass surveillance of communications in transit
  • Enables ISPs to fit equipment to facilitate surveillance
  • Enables to monitor peoples internet activities
