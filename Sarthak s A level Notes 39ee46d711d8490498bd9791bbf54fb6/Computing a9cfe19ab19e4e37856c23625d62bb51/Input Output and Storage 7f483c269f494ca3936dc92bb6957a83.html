Input, Output and Storage

Input Devices

Barcodes

There are two types of barcodes:

  • Linear

    Linear barcodes are often used to mark products in supermarkets

  • 2D

    2D barcodes are able to hold more information and are used for URLs and ticket less entry

Barcode Readers

There are four main types:

  • Pen-type readers

    In pen-type readers a light source and photo diode are placed next to each other in the tip of a pen. The diode measures the intensity of light reflected back and generates a wave form that is used to measure the widths of the bars and spaces of the barcode

  • Laser scanners

    Laser scanners, work similar to pen-type just with a laser beam as the light source. They are reliable and economical for low volume applications

  • CCD readers

    CDD or CMOS (Complementary Metal Oxide Semiconductor) sensor is comprimised of millions of tiny light sensors arranged in a grid.

  • Camera based readers

    Camera-based imaging scanner used a camera an image processing techniques to decode a 1D or 2D barcode. It can read a barcode on any surface, on media or on a screen. It can also read it if the barcode is damaged.

Digital Cameras

These have millions of light sensors arranged in a grid. binary data from each sensor is recorded onto the cameras memory card so that the image can be reproduced using suitable software at a computer.

Radio Frequency Identification (RFID)

This is used as both input and output - an input to read the signal, and output to transmit a signal from an active tag.

They can be used to track objects over 300 meters away and do not need a line of sight.

An RFID chip consists of a small microchip transponder and an antenna. The micro chip is around 1mm while the antenna is much larger. This can be embedded into small capsules and injected under the skin in animals.

Active tags are physically larger as they include a battery to power the tag so they actively transmit a signal to be picked up.

Passive tags are cheaper to produce as they do not have a battery, they rely on an electromagnetic power to charge the card using the coiled antenna. Once powered it sends its data to the reader nearby.

Output Devices

Screens

  • LCD

    Liquid crystal display moniters contain groups of red, green and blue dioides to form each pixel.

    • They reach their maxium brightness immediately
    • The image is much shaper
    • The LEDs are smaller so the screen can be thinner
    • They are more reliable
  • Organic LED (OLED)

    The screen works similar to LCD but are made of plastic so they are more flexible.

    • They are flexible
    • They are thinner
    • They are consume less power
    • Faster refresh rates
    • They have truer colours

Printers

  • Laser

    They offer high quality, high speed printing. Its main use is for text heavy documents

  • Inkjets

    Inkjets work by by spraying minute dots of ink onto paper to create an image

  • Dot matrix

    These are known as impact printer. These printers are more robust but produce a worse image

  • 3D printers

    They use a filament that is melted and placed on a plane that has a x,y,z axis

Storage Devices

Hard Disks (HDD)

A hard disk uses a magnetic plate that s either polarised (north or south) to represent 1 or 0. The disk is divided into concentric circles, and then divided into sectors. A head is used to read the data on the hard disk. They are relatively slow but are able to store high capacities.

Optical Disks

There are 3 main formats: read-only, recordable and rewrite-able.

An optical disk works by using a high powered laser to burn sections of the disk. And a sensor is used to see if a bit reflects light or not.

  • Read-only

    CD-ROM

    These contain pits and lands, pits cause light to be scatted

    There is only one track, so it is arranged as a tight spiral

  • Re-write

    DVD-RW

    These use a laser and a magnet to set the data. The laser heats the surface while the magnet sets it to 1 or a 0.

Optical disks are cheap but easily damaged

Solid-state Disk (SSD)

Solid-state memory consists of millions of NAND flash memory cells, and a controller that manages the data.

Each cell works by delivering a current along the bit and word line to activate the flow of electrons from the source to the drain. The current is strong enough to let a few electrons past into a floating gate where they are trapped. No charge is considered 1 and charge is considered 0.

SSD are smaller and much more portable, but they are more expensive

RAM & ROM

  • RAM

    It is used to store programs and data that are currently being used. It is volatile, so data is lost when the computer switches off

  • ROM

    Hold data that needs to be permanently stored. It contains the boot up sequence for the computer and holds the BIOS.
