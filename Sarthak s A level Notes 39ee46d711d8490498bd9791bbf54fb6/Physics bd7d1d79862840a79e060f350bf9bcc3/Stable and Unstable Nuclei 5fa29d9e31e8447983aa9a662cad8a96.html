Stable and Unstable Nuclei

A stable isotope has a nuclei that does not disintegrate, so there must be a force holding them together. The two forces are the strong nuclear and electrostatic force. The strong nuclear forces holding it together while the electrostatic force pushes it apart.

The Strong Nuclear Force

  • Its range is no more then 3-4 fentometers
  • It has the same effect between two protons as it does between two neutrons or a proton and a neutron
  • It has an attractive force down to 0.5 fentometers then it becomes a repulsive force preventing particles from being pushed into each other.

The Existence of a Neutrino and Antineutrino

Neutrinos where fist hypothesised due to observation of beta decay. Scientists originally thought that the only particle being emitted was an electron, however energy after was less then before, which didn't fit with the principal of conservation of mass.

Wolfgang Pauli suggested another particle was being emitted too and carried away the missing energy.

It had to have 0 charge and to have almost 0 mass. This was then accepted after various other discoveries, and the neutrino was eventually observed 25 years later.
