Forced Vibrations and Resonance

Forced Vibrations

Imagine pushing someone on a swing at regular intervals. If each push is timed suitably the swing goes higher and higher. These pushes are called periodic force which is a force being applied at regular intervals.

  • When the system oscillates without a periodic force being applied to it, the system's frequency is called its natural frequency.
  • When a periodic force is applied to the system, the response depends on the frequency on the periodic force. The system undergoes forces vibrations when a periodic force is applied to it.

Resonance

When the system is oscillating at the maximum amplitude, the phase difference between displacement and periodic force is 90˚.

The periodic force is then exactly in phase with the velocity of the system, and the system is in resonance. The frequency at the maximum amplitude is called the resonant frequency.

The lighter the damping:

  • the larger the maximum amplitude becomes at resonance, and
  • the closer the resonant frequency is to the natural frequency of the system

As the applied frequency becomes increasingly larger than the resonant frequency of the mass-spring system,

  • the amplitude of the oscillations decrease more and more
  • the phase difference between the displacement and the periodic force increases from 90˚ until the displacement is at 180˚ out of phase with the periodic force

For an oscillating system that is free, the applied frequency = the natural frequency
