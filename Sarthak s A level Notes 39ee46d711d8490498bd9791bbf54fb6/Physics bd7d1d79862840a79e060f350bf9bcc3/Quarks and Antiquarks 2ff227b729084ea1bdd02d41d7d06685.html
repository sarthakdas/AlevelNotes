Quarks and Antiquarks

The properties of hadrons, such as charge, strangeness and rest mass can be explained by assuming they are composed if smaller particles known as quarks and antiquarks. There are 3 types of quarks and they each have their corresponding antiquark.

Up Quarks (U)

Charge +2/3

Strangeness 0

Down (D)

Charge -1/3

Strangeness 0

Strange (S)

Charge -1/3

Strangeness -1

The antiquark version has the opposite charges of Charge and Strangeness.

Quark Combinations

Baryons

Baryons are made up of 3 quarks or 3 antiquarks

Mesons

Mesons are made up of 1 quark and 1 antiquark

Kaons always include one strange quark

Quark and Beta Decay

In ß- decay, a neutron changes into a proton. In terms of quarks a D quark changes to a U quark.

In ß+ decay, a proton changes into a neutron. In terms of quarks a U quark changes into a D quark
