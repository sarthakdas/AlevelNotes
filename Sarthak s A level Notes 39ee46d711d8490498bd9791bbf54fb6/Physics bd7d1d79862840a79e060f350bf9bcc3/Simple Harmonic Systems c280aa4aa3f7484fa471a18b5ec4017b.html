Energy and Simple Harmonic Motion

Free Oscillations

A free oscillating object oscillates with a constant amplitude because there is no friction. The only force acting o the object is the restoring force. The total energy in this system stays the same even though the stores of this energy changes. From gravitational to kinetic energy.

Damped Oscillations

Oscillations of a simple pendulum gradually die away because air resistance gradually reduces the total energy in the system. The forces causing the amplitude to decrease are described as dissipative forces because they dissipate energy. The motion is said to be damped is dissipative forces are present.

Light Damping

Occurs when the time period is independent of the amplitude, however the amplitude gradually decreases, reducing by a fraction each cycle.

Critical Damping

It is when the object stops oscillating after it has been displaced and released. The system returns to equilibrium in the shortest possible time. Generally used in car suspension.

Heavy Damping

Occurs when the damping is so strong that the displaced object returns to equilibrium more slowly if it was critically damped. No oscillating motion occurs .
