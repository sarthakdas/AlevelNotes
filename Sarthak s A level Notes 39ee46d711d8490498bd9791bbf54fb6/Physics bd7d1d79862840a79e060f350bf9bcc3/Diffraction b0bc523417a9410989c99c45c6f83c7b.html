Diffraction

Diffraction is the spreading out of waves when they pass through a gap or by an edge.

Diffraction of water waves can be observed using a ripple tank. The waves spread out more if:

  • The gap is made narrower
  • The wavelength is made larger

Diffraction by Single Slit

This effect can be demonstrated by directing a parallel beam of light at the slit. The diffracted light forms a pattern that can be observed on a white screen. The patterns shows a central fringe with further fringes on the side of the central fringe. The fringe intensity is largest at the centre.

Note:

  • The central fringe is twice as wide as each of the outer fringes
  • The peak intensity of each fringe decreases with distance from the centre
  • Each of the outer fringes is the same width
  • The outer fringes are much less intense than the central fringe

Derivation of Diffraction Law

Forming the triangle QPY with angle <QYP being 90.

The distance QY is equal to n. Where is the wavelength and n is the number of times it is in that space

Using sin rule sin = QY/QP. Also when QP=d and QY=n

Which can be rearranged to d sin = QY = n

Uses of Diffraction Grating

Diffraction gratings are commonly used in spectrometers to study the spectrum of light. They can be used to identify different types of elements.
