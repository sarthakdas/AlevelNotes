Wave Particle Duality

Light is part of the electromagnetic spectrum of waves. The theory of electromagnetic waves predicted predicted waves beyond visible light. However light did not seem to follow the photoelectric effect

Light has a dual nature, in that it can behave as a wave or as a particle.

Wave like Nature

It is observed during diffraction of light takes place. This happens for example when light passes through a narrow slit. The emerging light spreads out in the same was a water waves spread.

Particle like Nature

It is observed in the photoelectric effect. When light is directed at a metal surface and an electron at the surface absorbs a photon of frequency.

Matter waves

Electrons in a beam can be deflected by having a magnetic field. This is evidence that electrons have a particle like nature. The idea that matter particles also have a wave like nature was considered by Louis de Brogile.

Therefor the hypothesis is that:

  • Matter particles have a dual wave-particle nature
  • The wave like behaviour of a matter particle is characterised bu a wavelength

Evidence for de Broglie's Hypothesis

A narrow beam of electrons in a vacuum is directed at a thin metal foil. A metal is composed of many tiny crystalline regions. Each region or grain consists of positive ions. The row of atoms cause the electrons in the beam to be diffracted, just as a beam of light is diffracted when it passes through a slit.

The electrons in the beam pass through the metal foil and are diffracted in certain directions only. They form a ring pattern on the fluorescent screen at the end of the tube. Each ring is due to electrons diffracted by the same amount of grains at the same angle of incident beam.

The wave length for electrons accelerated in a vacuum tube is about the smae size as electromagnetic waves in the X-ray part of the spectrum

If particles with greater mass were traveling at the same speed as the electrons, they would show a more tightly packed diffraction pattern, because mass and momentum is greater.
