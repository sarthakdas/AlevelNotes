Progressive Waves

Waves that pass through a substance are vibrations that pass through the substance, creating areas of compression and rarefaction. These are mechanical waves.

Electromagnetic waves are vibrating electric and magnetic fields that progress through space with out the need for a substance.

Amplitude

The maximum displacement of a vibrating particle (the maximum height of the wave)

Frequency

The number of cycles of vibration of a particle per second

Wave Length

The least distance between two adjacent vibrating particles (distance between two crests)

Speed

How fast the speed is moving

Phase

Two waves with the same frequency where they line up can be called in 'phase' if they do not line up they are out of 'phase'

Phase Diffrence

It is the faction of cycles between the vibrations of two particles, measured in degrees or radians.
