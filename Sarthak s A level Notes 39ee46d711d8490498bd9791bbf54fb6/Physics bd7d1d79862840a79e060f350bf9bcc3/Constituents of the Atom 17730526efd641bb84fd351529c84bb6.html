Constituents of the Atom

We cannot see atoms, but we know form Rutherford’s alpha-scattering investigation, that every atom contains:

  • A positively charged nucleus composed of protons and neutrons
  • Electrons that surround a nucleus

We can use the word nucleon for a proton or a neutron in the nucleus. Nucleon number refers to the atomic mass.

Each electron has a negative charge, while protons have a positive charge, therefore the atom is held in by the electrostatic force of attraction. Rutherford’s investigations also proved that the nucleus contains most of the mass of an atom.

In the following table notice that:

  • The electron has a smaller mass than the proton or the neutron
  • The proton and the neutron have almost equal mass

Isotopes

Every atom of a given element has the same number of protons. The proton number is usually called the atomic number. Atoms of elements with different number of neutrons are called isotopes

Isotopes are atoms with the same number of protons and different numbers of neutrons

The total number of protons and the neutrons in an atom is called nucleon or sometimes the mass number of an atom. A nucleon is a neutron or a proton in the nucleus.

Each type of nucleus is called a nuclide.

Specific charge

The specific charge of a charged particle is defined as its charge divided by its mass. We can calculate the specific charge of a charged particle if we know the charge and the mass of the particle.
