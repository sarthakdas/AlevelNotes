Interference

Interference occurs when two sources of a coherent wave with different path difference meet on a screen causing they waves to interact with each other.

Coherent Waves - A wave with the same wavelength and frequency

Path Difference - How much longer one wave has traveled compared to another

Young's Double-Slit Experiment

To see two interference with light, two closely spaced parallel slits (double slit) using a suitable light source.

The light used must be monochromatic light so that the wavelength is the same.

The two slits act as coherent sources of light, which means they emit waves with a constant phase difference and frequency.

The fringes seen are parallel and evenly spaced, to the double slits.

Note: If the single slit is too wide

If it is too wide then each part it produces a fringe pattern, which is displaced slightly from the pattern due to adjacent parts of the single slit. As a result, the dark fringes of the double slit pattern become narrower then the bring fringes, and the contrast is lost.

Where the bright fringe forms, the light from one slit reinforces the light from the other slit. In other words they are in phase with each other.

Where the dark fringe forms, the light from one slit cancels the light from the other slit. In other words they are not in phase (180˚ degrees out)

Interference Patterns with White Light

In the double slit experiment the fringe separation depends on the colour of light used. White light is composed of a continuous spectrum of colours. Each colour has its own wave length.

The central pattern is white because every-colour contributes at the centre pattern.

The inner fringes are tinged with blue on the inner side and red on the outer-side. This is because the red fringes are more spaced out then the blue fringes.

The outer fringes merge into an indistinct background of white light becoming fainter with increasing distance from the centre.

Saftey with Lasers

When working with lasers the lasers must never be looked at directly not even through reflection. Also proper signage must be used around where it is used.
