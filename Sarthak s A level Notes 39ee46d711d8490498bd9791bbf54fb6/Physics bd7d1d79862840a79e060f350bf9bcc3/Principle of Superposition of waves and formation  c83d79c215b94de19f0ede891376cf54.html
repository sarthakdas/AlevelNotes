Principle of Superposition of waves and formation of Stationary Waves

Stationary Waves

Stationary waves are formed when two progressive wave of the same frequency travel in opposite directions.

This can be achieved on a string in tension by fixing both ends and making the middle vibrate, so progressive waves travel towards each end, reflect at the ends, and then pass through each other.

Stationary waves vibrate feely but do not transfer energy to their surroundings.

There is no displacement at the nodes and the greatest displacement at the anti-nodes. Since they are at fixed position no energy is transferred.

Pitch

The pitch of a note corresponds to frequency:

  • Raising the tension or shortening the length increases the pitch
  • Lower the tension or increasing the length lowers the pitch

Constructive and Destructive Superposition

When waves are in phase they reinforce each other to produce a larger wave. When they are out of phase they cancel each other out.
