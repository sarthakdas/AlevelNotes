Scalars and Vectors

Any from of measurement can be split into two categories: scalars and vectors. Scalars do not have a direction and only have a magnitude, such as speed; whereas vectors have a direction and magnitude, like velocity.

A vector is any physical quantity that has a direction as well as magnitude

A scalar is any physical quantity that is not directional

Scalar

  • Speed
  • Mass
  • Temperature
  • Distance

Vector

  • Velocity
  • Weight
  • Acceleration
  • Displacement

Representation of Vectors

Vectors can be represented using straight lines arrows which indicates direction, and the length of the line representing the magnitude.

Addition of Vectors

The original vectors, where there are multiple vectors on a object are called component vectors

The final vectors, where multiple vectors are combined is called resultant vectors

When vectors are at right angles they can be added together
