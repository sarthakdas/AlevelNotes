Classification of Particles

Hadrons

They are particles that feel the strong nuclear force

Baryons

All baryons decay into protons

Baryons include protons and neutrons. They are made up of 3 quarks or 3 antiquarks

Baryon number is a quantum number, meaning that it can only be an integer

Mesons

All mesons are unstable

There are 2 types Kaons and Pions. They were discovered in cosmic rays. They are observed in a cloud chamber to see their paths.

They are made up of one quark and one antiquark

Kaons decay into Pions

Pions are the exchange particles in strong nuclear force

Leptons

They are particles that don't feel the strong nuclear force

They are fundamental particles and only interact with other particles using the weak nuclear force.

These include Electrons, Electron-Neutrino, Muon, Muon-Neutrino. They are made up of one quark and one antiquark. All muons have a lepton

Muons decay into electrons. While Neutrons do not react at all unless by weak nuclear force.

Lepton-electron and Lepton-muon numbers should be counted separately.

Strange Particles

Kaons are produced during strong interaction, but decay using weak interaction, this property and many others let them to be called the strange particle.

  • Kaons decay into Pions, Muons, Antineutrinos, Antimuons and Neutrinos.
  • Charged Pions decay into Muons and Antineutrinos, or Antimuons and Neutrinos.
  • Muons and Antimuons decay into Electrons and Antineutrinos, or Positrons and Neutrinos.

Strangeness is also a quantum number.

Strangeness is only conserved during strong interactions.
