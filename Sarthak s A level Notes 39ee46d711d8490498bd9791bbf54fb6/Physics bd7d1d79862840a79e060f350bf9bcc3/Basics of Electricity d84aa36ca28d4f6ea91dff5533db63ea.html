Basics of Electricity

The electric current is the rate of flow of charge in a wire or component

The current is due to the passage of charged particles. These charged particles are referred to as charged carriers

  • In a metal the charged carriers are electrons
  • In a solution the charged carriers are the ions

The convention for the direction of current in a circuit is from positive to negative

Current

It is defined as the magnetic force between two parallel wires when they carry the same current

Charge

It is defined as the charge flow in one second when the current is one ampere

Materials can be classified as conductors, insulators or semi conductors.

  • In an insulator each electron is attached to an atom and cannot move away from the atom
  • In a conductor some electrons are free so when a potential difference is applied the electrons move
  • In a semiconductor the number of charged carriers increase with temperature

Potential Difference

It is defined as the work done per unit charge

It is the amount of energy required per unit charge

The emf of a source of electricity is defined as the electrical energy produced per unit charge passing through the source.

  • In a device that has resistance the work done is transferred as thermal energy, because charge carriers collide with each other causing vibration and heat
  • In a motor the work done is transferred as kinetic energy of the motor
  • For a loud speaker the work done is transferred as sound energy

Power

Resistance

It is the measure of difficulty of making current pass through the component. It is caused by the collision between charge carriers.
