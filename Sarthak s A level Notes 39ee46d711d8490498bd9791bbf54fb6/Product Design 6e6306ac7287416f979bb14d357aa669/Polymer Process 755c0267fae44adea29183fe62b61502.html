Polymer Process

Vacuum Forming

It is used to make 3D products such as lightweight trays. It uses thermo plastics which is heated then sucked over a mould causing the plastic to take the shape of the mould.

  • The mould is placed on the bed of the machine
  • Polymer sheet is clamped over the mould and heated
  • The mould is raised into the mould
  • The vacuum pump is turned on which sucks the polymer into the mould

Thermoforming

Similar to vacuum forming except and additional mould is pressed onto the surface giving extra detailing.

Calendering

It is a smoothing and rolling process used to make paper generally. Pellets are heated till a dough like consistency and then extruded through a series of heated rollers becoming squashed and stretched to make it thinner.

Line Bending

It is the process to bend sheets of thermoplastics. An electrically heated part provides heat used to make products such as shelves. Suitable for one off or batch production, as it is slow and labour intensive.

Lamination (Lay-up)

It is used to make 3D objects from fibre-based composites such as kayaks and boats.

  • Mould is coated in release agent such as PVA
  • Top layer of gel coat is applied containing the pigment
  • Fibre-glass is cut to size and laid over the mould and brushed with a roller to remove any air bubbles
  • This is repeated till desired thickness is achieved

Injection Moulding

Used to manufacture complex shapes such as electrical casings. Made by meting the polymer and moulding it under high pressure.

  • Pellets are poured into the hopper
  • A screw with heater melts the pellets and moves them
  • When enough plastic is melted it is injected into the mould
  • Once water cooled the mould is ejected and removed

Blow Moulding

Commonly used to produce plastic bottles.

  • Polymer is fed into a hopper
  • Screw pulls the plastic through a heated section and extruded
  • Hot air is blown into the plastic causing it to expand and take the shape of the mould

Rotational Moulding

Used to produce heavy duty, seamless hollow objects such as kayaks.

  • Polymer is loaded into a mould and clamped
  • The mould is then taken to an oven
  • It is then spun slowly (around 20 rotations a minute)
  • When it cools down the polymer shrinks allowing the mould to be removed

Extrusion

This produces a solid rod/tube with the same cross sectional shape e.g 'T' or 'H' shapes. This works similar to injection moulding.

Compression Moulding

Used to make electrical fittings mainly.
