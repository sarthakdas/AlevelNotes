Classification of Materials

Materials of product design are generally split into groups or classifications:

  • Metal
  • Wood
  • Polymers
  • Paper and Boards
  • Composites
  • Smart Materials
  • Modern Materials

Some materials have more then one classifications, for example metals can be classified as ferrous, non ferrous or alloy.

Metals

Ferrous

A metal containing mostly of iron and carbon, they are magnetic and will rust

Low carbon steel, Medium carbon steel, Cast Iron

Non-Ferrous

A metal that does not contain iron, they are not magnetic and will not rust

Aluminium, Copper, Zinc, Tin

Allow

A metal made out of two or more elements. Alloys can also be sub-classified as ferrous alloys or non-ferrous alloys.

Stainless steel, Die steel, Brass, Pewter

Woods

Hardwood

A wood from broad-leafed (deciduous) trees. These are generally slow growing and lose leaves in autumn.

Oak, Ash, Mahogany, Teak

Softwood

A wood from a coniferous (cone bearing) tree. These are generally are are fast growing and tend to be evergreens.

Pine, Spruce, Douglas fir, Redwood

Manufactured Boards

A man-made wood based composite material. They are available in much larger sized then solid woods.

Plywood, Chipboard, MDF

Polymers

Thermoplastic

A material which can be repeatedly reheated and reshaped, allowing for it to be recycled after its initial use. Thermoplastics have long linear chain molecules held by van der Walls forces.

LDPE, ABS, PVC

Thermosetting

A material which when heated undergoes a chemical change whereby the molecules form rigid cross links. Thermosetting polymers cannot be reheated and reshaped.

UF. MF, Epoxy resin

Elastomer

A material which at room, temperature can deform under pressure and then upon release of the pressure, will return to its original shaped. They have weak bonds that allow them to stretch easily.

Natural rubber, Ploybutadiene, Silicone

Paper and Board

Paper and board are compliant materials that can be scored, folded, and cut with basic tools.

Layout paper, Tracing paper, Bleed paper

Composites

A material compromised of two or more different materials, resulting in a material with enhanced properties.

Carbon fibre, Concrete, Glulam

Smart materials

A material whose physical properties change in response to an input or change in the environment. Such as electricity, pressure or temperature

SMA, Piezo electric material, Photo-chromatic pigment

Modern materials

A material developed through the invention of new or improved processes.

Kevlar, PMC, Ploymorph
