Polymers

Majority of polymers are made via the fractional distillation of crude oil which produced fraction containing a mixture of hydrocarbons. Which is then converted to a polymer by either polymerisation or polycondensation.

Thermoplastics

  • Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE)

    Very tough, weather-proof, low level of rigidity

    Squeeze detergent bottles, Toys, Carrier bags

  • High Density Polyethylene (HDPE)

    Weather-proof, tough, translucent

    Chemical drums, jerry cans

  • Polypropylene (PP)

    Translucent, good fatigue resistance(hinge property)

    Rope, folders, food containers

  • High Impact Polystyrene (HIPS)

    Hard, Rigid, translucent

    Yoghurt pots, Single use drink cups

  • Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS)

    Extremely tough, hard, Opaque

    Telephone handsets, Rigid luggages

  • Polymethyl-methacrylate (PMMA)

    Tough, hard, good chemical resistance, translucent

    Car light casings, Lighting covers

  • Nylon

    Tough, corrosion resistant, low friction

    Bearings, Gears, Cable ties

  • Rigid Polyvinyl Chloride (uPVC)

    Rigid, Opaque, fire retardant

    Window frames, Guttering, Bank cards

  • Flexible Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC)

    Tough, Flexible

    Hose pipes, Cable insulation, Medical grade tubing, Inflatable products

Thermoset Polymers

  • Urea Formaldehyde (UF)

    Hard, heat resistant, brittle

    Electrical Fittings, Adhesives

  • Melamine Formaldehyde (MF)

    Hard, opaque, tough

    Decorative laminates, Picnic ware

  • Polyester Resin

    Rigid, heat resistant, brittle

    Castings, car parts, chair seats

  • Epoxy Resin

    Rigid, clear, hard, tough

    Adhesives, Surface coatings
