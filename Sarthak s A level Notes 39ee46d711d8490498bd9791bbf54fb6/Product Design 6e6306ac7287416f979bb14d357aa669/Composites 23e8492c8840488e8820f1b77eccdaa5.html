Composites

A composite is a material compromised of two or more materials resulting in a material with enhanced properties.

Carbon Fibres mixed with Polyester or Epoxy Resin (CFRP)

Lightweight, corrosion resistant, tough, hard, low thermal expansion

Sports equipment, Finishing rods, Prosthetics

Glass Fibres mixed with Polyester Resin (GRP)

Lightweight, tough, hard, good compressive strength

Boat hulls, Pond liners, Kayak shells

Tungsten Carbide

Hard, tough, resistant to high temperatures

Cutting tools, Lathe tools

Aluminium Composite Board

Lightweight, rigid, tough, malleable

Sound-proofing panels in cars, Signage

Concrete

High compression strength, consistency across structure

Pathways, Beams, Blocks

Reinforced Concrete

Same as concrete, fire resistant

Buildings, Bridges, Grid Floors

Fibre Cement

Lighter in weight than reinforced concrete, hard, tough, good at low temperatures

Suspended floors, Complex geometric shapes

Engineered Wood (Glulam)

Good aesthetics, natural alternative to reinforces concretes

Beams, Bridges, Domes
