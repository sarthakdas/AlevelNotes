Metal Enhancements

Each type of metal ha a range of properties such as toughness, hardness or ductility. These properties can be changed using heat treatments.

Work Hardening

When metal is 'cold worked' such as bending, rolling or hammering the crystals are distorted and changed. This increases tensile strength and hardness. When the crystals are distorted they can not move around freely, as a result become less ductile.

The effects of work hardening can be removed by annealing.

Annealing

The metal is heated and then slowly cooled, allowing crystals to grow and slowly move into place. Done in industry sing a temperature controlled furnace, and in schools using the brazing hearth.

Case Hardening

It is the process for hardening the surface of steels with less than 0.4 per cent carbon content. The surface has greater hardness but the inner core still has softer properties. This improves wear resistance and is used mainly on gears.

Hardening and Tempering

Hardening is the process of heating medium and high carbon steels to alter the crystal structure and then quenching them in water, oils or salt. This increase harness and brittleness

Tempering is the heat treatment carried out after hardening to reduce extra hardness and increase toughness. The metal is heated to below the critical point (temperature where atoms of carbon and steel mix freely) and then slowly air cooled.
