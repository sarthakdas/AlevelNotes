Polymer Enhancements

Polymers are generally inert and will resit most chemical, insect and fungal attack. Certain polymer characteristics can be enhanced using additives which offer several advantages such as improved properties.

Additives to Improve Manufacturing

Lubricants

The addition of a wax or calcium reduces the viscosity of the molten polymer, making it less sticky. This allows it to flow into more intricate shapes and reduces moulding temperature

Thermal antioxidants

These help to prevent the polymer oxidising or discolouring due to excessive heat during processing.

Additives to Improve Aesthetics

Pigments

These are tiny particles that are mixed into the polymer in its molten state to give colour to the final processed product.

Additives to Improve Product Function

Antistatic

This improves the surface conductivity by attracting moisture from the surroundings, therefore reducing the static charge.

Flame Retardants

This reduces the likely hood of combustion or the spread of fire.

Plasticisers

This allows plastics to become less hard and brittle at normal temperatures. They also allow the polymers to be formed at lower temperatures.

Fillers

They provide the bulk of the product to reduce the polymer used and improve the properties of the product.

Additives to Prolong Life

Antioxidants

Help reduce the degradation when exposed to oxygen, however increases brittleness and pigment discolouration.

UV Light Stabilisers

Prevents the polymer chains from breaking down when in the sunlight.

Additives to Encourage Degradation

Biodegradable Plasticisers

This makes it more flexible, softer, and easier to break down.

Bio-batch Additive

An additive that dictates what way the material breaks down.
