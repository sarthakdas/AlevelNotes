Wood Enhancement

Natural wood can have defects such as splits and knots, which effect stability. Natural wood as a manufacturing material has several disadvantages:

  • Strength decreases when wet
  • Highly combustible
  • Susceptible to fungal / insect attack
  • It is anisotropic (different properties in different direction due to grain)

Enhancements using Preservatives

Fungal Attacks

Copper preservatives are used as it is naturally occurring, pigments are also added to improve aesthetics.

Fire Retardent

The preservatives with these properties are pressure treated into the wood.

Hardness

Wood is impregnated using polysaccharides, which cures with in the wood cell structure.

Resins and Laminations

The wood industry used engineered wood to enhance the properties of wood.

Manufactured Boards

Such as chipboard are made from compressing wood chips in resin such as urea formaldehyde. This increases stability and strength. The resin generally contains other additives such as fire retardants.

Engineered Lumber

Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) and Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL) are made made by layering veneer or stands of wood with resins. They are then heat pressed curing them. These woods are less prone to warping and splitting.
